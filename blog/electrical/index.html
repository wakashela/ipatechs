Power Up Your Operations — Electrical Engineering Products & Solutions

November 5, 2024 · 8 Min Read

Reliable electrical infrastructure is the backbone of any productive industrial facility. Power interruptions, motor failures, or switchgear malfunctions don't just cause downtime — they create safety risks and cost your operation thousands. IPATECHS provides a comprehensive range of electrical engineering components to keep your plant running safely and efficiently.

Electric Motors & Variable Speed Drives

We supply AC and DC motors from leading manufacturers including ABB, Siemens, and Mitsubishi Electric — covering IEC frame sizes from fractional kW up to multi-megawatt industrial drives. Our variable frequency drives (VFDs) enable precise speed control, dramatically reducing energy consumption in pump, fan, and compressor applications.

  • IE3 and IE4 high-efficiency motors
  • Explosion-proof motors for hazardous areas (ATEX/IECEx certified)
  • Soft starters and direct-on-line starters
  • Variable frequency drives (VFDs) from 0.37kW to 2,800kW

Switchgear & Protection Equipment

Protecting your electrical assets starts with the right switchgear. We supply moulded case circuit breakers (MCCBs), miniature circuit breakers (MCBs), contactors, and overload relays from ABB, Schneider Electric, and Rockwell Automation. Our protection devices are sized and coordinated to your system's fault levels.

Cables, Conduits & Cable Management

A well-organised cable infrastructure reduces maintenance time and improves safety. We supply armoured and unarmoured power cables, control cables, cable trays, conduit systems, and terminal blocks. All our cable products meet IEC standards and are rated for the temperature and chemical environments common in East African industry.

Transformers & Power Quality

From distribution transformers to power factor correction capacitor banks and harmonic filters, IPATECHS helps you optimise your power quality, reduce utility penalties, and protect sensitive equipment from voltage fluctuations common in the Tanzanian grid.
