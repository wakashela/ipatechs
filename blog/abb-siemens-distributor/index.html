Authorised Distributors vs Grey Market Resellers

This distinction is critical and frequently misunderstood. An authorised distributor has a formal, contractual relationship with the manufacturer — ABB or Siemens — that includes technical training, access to factory support, warranty backing, and preferred pricing. The manufacturer vets authorised distributors for technical competence, financial stability, and market reputation. Authorised distributors receive regular product updates, have access to factory engineering resources for complex application support, and can process warranty claims directly through the manufacturer's system. Grey market resellers, by contrast, source products through unofficial channels — often purchasing excess stock from projects in other countries, buying from authorised distributors in other territories, or acquiring products of uncertain provenance. Grey market products may be genuine but are typically not covered by local manufacturer warranty, may not have local technical support, and may not carry the correct certifications for East African electrical standards. More concerningly, some grey market resellers knowingly or unknowingly sell counterfeit products — imitation circuit breakers that fail to trip under fault conditions, fake PLC modules that fail unpredictably in service, or cloned drives that lack critical safety features.

Warranty and Support Considerations

When you purchase ABB or Siemens products through an authorised channel, you receive full manufacturer warranty coverage — typically 12–24 months from shipment for standard products, extendable for project-specific agreements. In the event of a defect, the authorised distributor manages the warranty claim process, including technical investigation, manufacturer liaison, and replacement logistics. If your plant is in Mwanza and a drive fails under warranty, your authorised distributor should be able to ship a warranty replacement within days, not weeks. Grey market channels typically offer no manufacturer warranty at all — the reseller may offer their own "warranty" but has no ability to process a manufacturer claim, meaning defective products become the buyer's problem.

How to Verify Authorised Distributor Status

Simple steps protect your organisation. Ask the supplier for their authorisation letter from ABB or Siemens — this is a formal document on the manufacturer's letterhead, signed by a country or regional manager, specifying the product ranges the distributor is authorised to sell. Contact the manufacturer's local or regional office directly to confirm the distributor's status. Check the manufacturer's website — both ABB and Siemens maintain online partner locators that list authorised distributors by country. Be wary of suppliers who say they "work with" or "have connections at" the manufacturer — this is not the same as being authorised. Lead Times and Availability

Standard catalogue items — common motor ratings, standard drive sizes, routine circuit breakers and contactors — should be available from local stock in Dar es Salaam. More specialised items — large medium-voltage motors, engineered drive systems, custom switchgear configurations — are typically built to order with lead times of 8–16 weeks from European or Asian factories. A well-connected authorised distributor can expedite these timelines through their direct relationship with the manufacturer's order desk and logistics team. They also have visibility of global stock inventory and can sometimes source from another region's warehouse faster than ordering new production.
