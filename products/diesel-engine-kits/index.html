Frequently Asked Questions

Genuine vs aftermarket engine parts — which should I choose?

Genuine OEM engine parts are manufactured to the exact specifications, metallurgy, and tolerances of the original component. Aftermarket parts, while often cheaper, frequently use inferior materials, looser tolerances, and lack the rigorous quality control of OEM production. In a diesel engine operating in Tanzania's heat, dust, and high-load conditions, the cost of a single premature failure from a non-genuine component far exceeds the upfront saving. We exclusively supply genuine OEM parts with full manufacturer warranty — because uptime is the only metric that matters.

How do I confirm engine part compatibility?

Send us your engine serial number (ESN) — found on the engine data plate, typically located on the valve cover or timing case. Our technical team cross-references the ESN against Cummins QuickServe, CAT SIS, and Volvo Penta EPC databases to identify every compatible part number for your specific engine build, including factory revisions, service bulletins, and superseded part numbers. We never guess from model year alone.

What break-in procedure should I follow after an overhaul?

A proper break-in procedure is essential to seat piston rings against freshly honed cylinder liners. We recommend: (1) pre-lube the turbocharger and prime the oil system before first start; (2) start and run at 1000–1200 RPM for 10 minutes to verify oil pressure and check for leaks; (3) operate at 50–75% load for the first 4–8 hours, varying engine speed — do not idle for extended periods; (4) change oil and filters after the first 50 hours to remove break-in wear metals; (5) gradually increase to full load over the next 100 hours. We supply the correct break-in oil, filters, and torque specifications for your specific engine model.
