Frequently Asked Questions

How do I identify counterfeit SKF bearings?

Genuine SKF bearings carry a hologram sticker, laser-etched markings, and a scannable QR code on the packaging. The surface finish is uniform, the rolling elements are mirror-polished, and the packaging box has clean, sharp printing. Counterfeits often have blurry logos, rough raceway surfaces, and poorly printed boxes. If the price seems too good to be true, it almost certainly is. We verify every bearing before it enters our inventory.

Large spherical roller bearings and special-clearance variants may require 3–4 weeks depending on manufacturing schedules.

Are SKF and FAG bearings interchangeable?

Technically yes for standard sizes — both manufacturers conform to ISO boundary dimensions, so an SKF 6205-2RS will fit where a FAG 6205-2RS was fitted. However, internal clearance, cage design, and lubricant specifications can differ. We recommend matching the original manufacturer unless a full engineering review confirms interchangeability without compromise to service life or performance.

What clearance class should I order for Tanzanian conditions?

C3 or C4 clearance is recommended for most Tanzanian industrial applications, particularly in high-temperature and dusty environments like cement kilns, sugar mills, and mining crushers. These higher clearance classes accommodate the thermal expansion that occurs when bearings operate in our ambient temperatures. Our engineers can advise on the correct clearance for your specific application, shaft fit, and housing design.
