Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my V-belts are correctly tensioned?

Under-tensioned belts slip, glaze, and overheat — while over-tensioned belts overload bearings and stretch prematurely. The correct tension produces a deflection of approximately 16 mm per metre of span under moderate thumb pressure. For critical drives, we recommend using a belt tension gauge (SKF or Gates Sonic Tension Meter) to set exact tension values. Our technicians can perform belt tension audits at your site and train your maintenance crew on proper tensioning procedure.

What lubrication do my roller chains need?

Roller chains require regular lubrication at the pin-bush interface to prevent elongation and wear. For slow-speed drives (below 3 m/s), manual or drip-feed lubrication with SAE 30-50 mineral oil is sufficient. For medium to high-speed drives (3-12 m/s), oil bath or pumped circulation lubrication is essential. We supply SKF chain oils with EP additives and anti-fling properties specifically formulated for industrial roller chain applications in dusty and humid East African environments.

When do I need a flexible coupling instead of a rigid one?

Flexible couplings are required whenever there is shaft misalignment (angular, parallel, or axial), shock loading, or torsional vibration between the driver and driven equipment. Rigid couplings transmit all misalignment forces directly into the bearings, causing premature failure. SKF Grid, Gear, and Jaw couplings accommodate up to 1.5 degrees of angular misalignment and absorb 30% of shock loads. Our engineers assess your application and specify the correct coupling type, size, and elastomeric element for your operating conditions.

Belt drive or chain drive — which should I choose?

Belt drives are preferred for high-speed applications (above 600 rpm driver speed), long centre distances, and where noise reduction is important. They require no lubrication and slip under overload — providing inherent motor protection. Chain drives are the correct choice when exact speed ratio must be maintained (no slip), operating temperatures exceed 80°C, or the environment has oil, grease, or chemical exposure that would degrade rubber belts. Our engineers evaluate your speed, torque, environment, and maintenance capability to recommend the right drive type.
