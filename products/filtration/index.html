What micron rating do I need for my hydraulic filter?

The correct micron rating depends on your hydraulic system's cleanliness target, which is determined by the most sensitive component in the circuit. Servo and proportional valves typically require 3-6 micron (beta 75+), standard directional control valves tolerate 10-12 micron, while gear pumps and cylinders can operate with 25 micron filtration. Our filtration engineers analyse your system's ISO 4406 cleanliness target, component sensitivity, and contamination ingression rate to specify the optimum filter element rating — too fine wastes money on premature element replacement; too coarse allows damaging particle ingression.

What are the correct filter change intervals for Tanzanian conditions?

Standard OEM service intervals assume clean European or North American operating conditions — not Tanzania's high-dust, high-humidity, and variable-fuel environments. We recommend reducing air filter service intervals by 30-40% in dusty mine and quarry operations, using restriction indicators rather than time-based replacement. Fuel filters should be changed at 250-hour intervals when using Tanzanian-sourced diesel due to higher particulate and water content. Engine oil filters should follow OEM recommendations but with oil analysis to confirm. Our specialists provide site-specific filter replacement schedules based on your actual operating conditions and contamination data.

Fleetguard Stratapore synthetic media lube filters maintain filtration efficiency throughout the full service interval, whereas cellulose aftermarket media degrades after 100-150 hours. The filter cost is negligible compared to the cost of a single engine rebuild or hydraulic pump replacement.
