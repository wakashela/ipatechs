Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose between Flygt N-series and B-series?

The N-series is designed for wastewater and sewage with its patented N-technology self-cleaning impeller that handles solids and fibrous materials without clogging. The B-series is designed for dewatering and drainage — pumping clean or solids-laden water from construction sites, mines, and flooded areas. If you're pumping raw sewage or sludge, choose N-series. If you're dewatering a foundation pit or mine, choose B-series. Our engineers confirm the selection against your actual fluid analysis and system head.

Dry-pit or wet-well installation — which is right?

Wet-well installation submerges the pump directly in the pumped fluid — standard for sewage pumping stations where the pump sits at the bottom of the collection sump. Dry-pit (or dry-installed) places the pump in a separate dry chamber, with the fluid piped to the pump suction. Dry installation gives easier access for maintenance but requires more civil works. Flygt N-series pumps support both configurations. We help evaluate your site constraints, available space, and maintenance access requirements to recommend the right approach.

What impeller material should I specify?

Flygt pumps offer impellers in grey cast iron (standard for clean or lightly contaminated water), Hard-Iron (high-chrome alloy for abrasive slurry and mine dewatering), and stainless steel (AISI 316 for corrosive fluids). For typical Tanzanian applications: municipal wastewater generally uses standard cast iron with N-technology hydraulics, mine dewatering at Geita and North Mara demands Hard-Iron for the abrasive tailings water, and saline or chemically aggressive applications require stainless steel. We specify the material based on your water analysis report.
