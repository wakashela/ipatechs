Frequently Asked Questions

What is the recommended replacement interval for spreader twistlocks?

Twistlock replacement intervals depend on duty cycle and operating conditions. For STS cranes handling 200,000+ container moves per year, we recommend inspecting twistlock mechanisms every 5,000 operating hours and replacing worn components at 15,000-20,000 hours as a preventive measure. Key inspection points include the twistlock pin body (check for deformation and cracking using dye penetrant), the flipper mechanism (check for free movement and spring tension), and the limit switch adjustment. Corrosive marine environments accelerate wear — cranes at Dar es Salaam Port typically require more frequent inspection cycles than inland depots. We supply complete twistlock rebuild kits for all Konecranes and Terex spreader models.

What crane wheel hardness specifications are required for port cranes?

Crane wheels for port applications are typically manufactured from forged alloy steel (42CrMo4 or 50CrMo4) with the tread and flange induction-hardened to 350-400 HB (38-43 HRC) at a minimum depth of 5mm. The wheel core remains at 220-250 HB for toughness and impact resistance. Hardness specification varies with wheel diameter and rail profile — larger wheels on STS gantry travel drives require deeper case hardening. All our crane wheels are supplied with ultrasonic testing reports confirming internal soundness and hardness traverse measurements across the tread profile. For heavily loaded RTG and STS crane wheels, we recommend wheels manufactured to DIN 15070 / FEM 1.001 standards.

What are the wire rope inspection and discard criteria for port cranes?

Wire rope inspection for port crane hoist applications follows ISO 4309. Key discard criteria: (1) number of visible broken wires in a length equal to 6× or 30× the rope diameter exceeds the standard limit (typically 5% of wires); (2) outer wire wear exceeding 40% of original wire diameter, even if no wires are broken; (3) any corrosion pitting, particularly internal corrosion in the valley between strands (requires opening the rope for inspection); (4) diameter reduction exceeding 7% from the nominal diameter; (5) any kink, birdcage, or structural distortion. For galvanized ropes in marine environments, inspect more frequently — every 3 months or 500 operating hours whichever comes first. We supply certified replacement wire ropes with full mill certificates including breaking load test reports.
