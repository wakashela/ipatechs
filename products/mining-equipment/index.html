Frequently Asked Questions

How long do crusher wear parts typically last?

Service life depends heavily on ore abrasiveness and crusher settings. In typical gold-ore processing at Tanzanian mines, manganese jaw plates last 300-800 operating hours, while cone mantles and bowl liners achieve 400-1000 hours. Highly abrasive quartz-rich ores reduce life significantly. Our engineers can analyse your wear patterns to recommend the optimal manganese grade — higher manganese content (Mn18 vs Mn14) increases work-hardening capacity and extends life in abrasive applications, though at higher initial cost.

Are your ground-engaging tools interchangeable with OEM specifications?

Yes. All our GET components — bucket teeth, adapters, cutting edges, and side cutters — are manufactured to the OEM dimensional specifications for each machine model. This ensures direct fitment without modification. Our aftermarket teeth use the same nose profiles and retention systems (vertical pin, horizontal pin, or J-series hammerless) as the original equipment. We supply GET for CAT, Komatsu, Hitachi, and Volvo excavators and wheel loaders.

Do you supply conveyor belt splicing services?

We supply all splicing materials — hot vulcanising kits and cold-bond adhesives — plus mechanical fasteners (Flexco, Mato) for steel-cord and fabric belts up to 1800mm width. For hot vulcanisation, we can arrange certified splicing technicians through our partner network in Tanzania. We recommend maintaining a splicing repair kit on-site for emergency belt repairs to minimise conveyor downtime. Our team can advise on the correct fastener size, belt preparation tools, and splice procedure for your specific belt type and tension requirements.

What are the recommended undercarriage maintenance intervals for dozers?

For CAT D8-D11 and Komatsu D155-D475 dozers operating in Tanzanian mining conditions, we recommend daily visual inspection of track tension, shoe bolt torque, and roller/idler condition. Track tension should be checked and adjusted every 250 operating hours — over-tensioning accelerates pin and bushing wear, while under-tensioning causes track derailment. Full undercarriage inspection including pin/bushing internal wear measurement, track link height measurement, and sprocket segment tooth wear assessment should be performed every 1,000 hours. We supply complete undercarriage replacement kits with all OEM-specification components.
