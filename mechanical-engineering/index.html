Industrial Valves for Every Process

Valves control the arteries of industry — steam, water, compressed air, fuel, chemicals — and a single failed valve can shut down an entire process line. IPATECHS supplies the full spectrum of industrial valves East Africa industries rely on:

  • Gate Valves — Rising-stem and non-rising stem designs in cast iron, cast steel, and stainless steel for on/off isolation in water, steam, and oil service. Available with flanged, threaded, and welded end connections up to Class 1500.
  • Globe Valves — For flow regulation and throttling service where precise control matters — boiler feedwater, cooling water bypass, and steam pressure-reducing stations.
  • Ball Valves — Quarter-turn isolation with full-bore and reduced-bore options. Floating ball designs in brass, carbon steel, and stainless steel. Trunnion-mounted ball valves for larger diameters and higher pressures. Fire-safe designs (API 607) for hydrocarbon service.
  • Butterfly Valves — Wafer, lug, and double-flanged body styles with EPDM, NBR, and PTFE seats. Triple-offset metal-seated butterfly valves for high-temperature steam and thermal oil applications.
  • Check Valves — Swing check, dual-plate wafer check, and piston-type lift check valves to prevent reverse flow and protect upstream equipment from backpressure damage.
  • Actuated Valves — Electric actuators from Rotork and Auma, pneumatic rack-and-pinion actuators, and manual gear operators — enabling remote operation, fail-safe positioning, and integration with plant DCS/SCADA systems.

Preventive Maintenance & Engineering Services

Beyond equipment supply, IPATECHS offers practical mechanical engineering services that extend asset life and reduce total cost of ownership:

  • Mechanical Seal Replacement — On-site replacement of pump mechanical seals including cartridge seals, split seals, and component seals. We work with Chesterton, John Crane, EagleBurgmann, and AESSEAL products, and we'll help diagnose the root cause of repeated seal failures — whether it's cavitation, dry running, solids abrasion, or incorrect flush-plan configuration.
