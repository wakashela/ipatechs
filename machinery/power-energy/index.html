Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between prime and standby rating?

Prime-rated gensets are designed to run as the primary power source for unlimited hours with variable load. Standby-rated gensets are for emergency backup during grid outages, typically limited to 200 hours per year with average load not exceeding 70% of rating. We help you select the correct rating.

Can you integrate solar with existing diesel generators?

Yes. We supply complete hybrid solar-diesel solutions with intelligent controllers that automatically manage solar penetration levels. Typical fuel savings range from 20–60% depending on solar resource and load profile.

Do you supply containerised or open-skid gensets?

Both. Containerised gensets offer weather protection, sound attenuation (down to 65 dBA at 1 m), and integrated fuel tanks. Open-skid gensets are suitable for installation inside existing plant rooms.

How do you handle synchronisation of multiple gensets?

We supply complete synchronising panels with Woodward/DeepSea/ComAp controllers that manage load sharing, kW and kVAR control, and automatic sequencing. Systems are pre-configured and tested before dispatch.

What about fuel consumption and running costs?

We provide detailed fuel consumption curves for every genset we quote, at 50%, 75%, and 100% load. For hybrid solar-diesel systems, we model expected fuel savings based on your site's solar irradiation data. Our team can also advise on bulk fuel storage and delivery logistics.
